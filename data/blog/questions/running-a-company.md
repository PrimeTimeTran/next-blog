# Introduction to EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System)

EOS is basically a **simple operating system for running a company**, especially small-to-mid sized orgs, using a shared structure for:

- vision
- people
- data
- issues
- process
- traction (execution)

---

## 🎯 1. Rocks

- 90-day priorities (NOT long-term goals)
- Usually 3–7 per person/team
- “What must get done this quarter”

---

## 📊 2. Scorecard

- Weekly numbers that show if the business is healthy
- Early warning system (not lagging KPIs)

---

## 🧠 3. IDS (Issue Solving Track)

- **Identify → Discuss → Solve**
- A structured way to handle problems in meetings without chaos

---

## 🧑‍💼 4. Level 10 Meeting

A very specific weekly meeting format:

- same agenda every time
- strict timing
- ends with “scorecard + IDS + rocks review”

---

## 🧭 5. V/TO (Vision/Traction Organizer)

This is the big one:

- company vision + strategy condensed into a few pages
- forces alignment across the org

Includes:

- core values
- core focus
- 10-year target
- marketing strategy
- 3-year picture
- 1-year plan
- quarterly rocks

---

## 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 6. Accountability Chart

- not an org chart
- focuses on **roles and responsibilities**
- “right seat / wrong seat” thinking

---

# 🧠 Why it felt memorable

EOS sticks in people’s heads because it:

- replaces vague management language with **fixed terms**
- enforces **rhythm + structure**
- turns company management into a repeatable “system”

# Conclusion

Understanding Waterfall, Agile, Scrum is important for the day to day functioning of a tech company.
However another methodology was needed for a company and all the problems which arise in any organization.

For example:

- Giving/Taking feedback regularly.
- Building trust between teammates.
- Defining short & long term goals.
- Preventing wasted time/energy on "scheduling" teammates to "last minute" meetings.
- Facilitating communication & transparency between different teams of an organization.
