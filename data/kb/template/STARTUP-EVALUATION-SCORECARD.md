> Use this to quickly evaluate whether to **join or start** a startup.

Score each category from **0–10**.

# 🧮 Final Score

$$
\text{Total Score} = \sum_{i=1}^{8} \text{Category}_i
$$

Max = **80**

---

# 📊 Interpretation

- **65–80** → Strong opportunity (worth serious consideration)
- **50–65** → Medium (depends on role/equity)
- **< 50** → High risk / weak fundamentals

---

# 🚨 Red Flags (Instant Filters)

If ANY of these are true, pause:

- No clear problem
- No user demand
- Founders vague or inconsistent
- No idea how to acquire users
- Equity structure unclear
- “We’ll figure it out later” mindset

---

# 🎯 High-Signal Questions (Use in Calls)

## Problem / Market

- “What evidence do you have that this problem exists?”
- “Who is currently paying for this?”

## Product

- “What traction do you have today?”
- “What changed recently that makes this possible now?”

## Growth

- “What’s your current user acquisition strategy?”
- “What’s working vs not working?”

## Business Model

- “How do you make money today?”
- “What does this look like at scale?”

## Team

- “Why are you the team to solve this?”
- “What have you built before?”

## Role (for you)

- “What would I own in the first 90 days?”
- “What does success look like in this role?”

---

# 🧠 Meta Insight

You are evaluating:

> **Market × Product × Team × Distribution × Timing**

Not just:

- idea
- hype
- brand (YC, etc.)

---

# 📌 Personal Filter (Final Step)

Ask yourself:

- Would I work on this for 2–5 years?
- Do I trust these people under pressure?
- Is the upside worth the risk?

---

# 🧭 Final Decision Rule

> If score is high AND you feel conviction → join

> If score is medium → negotiate harder (equity, role)

> If score is low → walk away
