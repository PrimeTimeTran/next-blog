# Linear Algebra

> Core idea: Math of vectors, transformations, and structure in space

---

# 1. Vectors

## Topics

- Vectors as lists of numbers
- Geometric interpretation (direction + magnitude)
- Vector addition & scalar multiplication

## Operations

- Dot product
- Norm (length)

## Why it matters

- Represents data, features, directions
- Foundation of geometry in higher dimensions

---

# 2. Vector Spaces

## Definition

- A set of vectors closed under addition and scalar multiplication

## Concepts

- Subspaces
- Span
- Linear combinations

## Why it matters

- Defines what “valid vector systems” look like
- Generalizes beyond 2D/3D space

---

# 3. Linear Independence

## Definition

- No vector can be written as a combination of others

# Related

- Basis
- Dimension

## Why it matters

- Minimal representation of information
- Avoid redundancy

---

# 4. Matrices

## Topics

- Matrix representation of data
- Matrix addition & multiplication

## Interpretation

- Matrix = transformation

## Why it matters

- Core tool for computations
- Encodes linear transformations

---

# 5. Systems of Linear Equations

## Topics

- Solving systems
- Augmented matrices

## Methods

- Gaussian elimination
- Row reduction

## Why it matters

- Modeling real-world constraints
- Core computational skill

---

# 6. Linear Transformations

## Definition

- Functions that preserve vector structure

## Properties

- Additivity
- Homogeneity

## Examples

- Rotations
- Scaling
- Reflections

## Why it matters

- Connects matrices to geometry
- Foundation for graphics & ML

---

# 7. Determinants

## Meaning

- Scalar value from a matrix

## Interpretation

- Measures scaling factor of transformation
- Indicates invertibility

## Why it matters

- Detects singular matrices
- Used in solving systems

---

# 8. Inverse Matrices

## Definition

- Matrix that “undoes” another

## Condition

- Only exists if determinant ≠ 0

## Why it matters

- Solving equations efficiently
- Reversing transformations

---

# 9. Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors

## Definition

- Special vectors that only scale under transformation

## Equation

- Av = λv

## Why it matters

- Stability analysis
- Principal component analysis (PCA)
- Core in ML and physics

---

# 10. Orthogonality

## Definition

- Vectors are perpendicular (dot product = 0)

## Concepts

- Orthonormal basis
- Projections

## Why it matters

- Simplifies computations
- Used in optimization and decompositions

---

# 11. Projections

## Idea

- Project one vector onto another

## Why it matters

- Least squares solutions
- Data approximation

---

# 12. Decompositions

## Types

- LU decomposition
- QR decomposition

## Why it matters

- Efficient computation
- Numerical stability

---

# Key Patterns (Connect to DSA / CS)

- Vectors → represent states/data
- Matrices → transformations/processes
- Independence → minimal representation
- Eigenvalues → dominant behavior
- Orthogonality → independence + simplification

---

# Geometric Intuition

- Vectors → arrows in space
- Matrices → actions on space
- Determinant → scaling of space
- Eigenvectors → directions that don’t rotate

---

# How Everything Connects

- Vectors form spaces
- Spaces have bases
- Matrices transform vectors
- Transformations have structure (eigenvalues)
- Orthogonality simplifies everything
