# Trigonometry Hub

## Purpose

This hub organizes the core concepts, functions, identities, and applications of trigonometry. Trigonometry connects angles, ratios, and circular motion, providing tools for solving problems in geometry, engineering, physics, and signal processing.

---

## Core Idea

Trigonometry is the study of relationships between angles and sides in triangles, extended to periodic functions that model oscillation and circular motion.

---

## Foundational Concepts

### Angles and Measurement

- **Degrees vs Radians** — Two systems for measuring angles
- **Unit Circle** — Radius 1 circle centered at origin; foundation for [[core-functions|trig functions]]
- **Reference Angles** — Simplifying angles outside 0-360° range
- **Angle Relationships** — Complementary, supplementary, coterminal angles

### Right Triangles

- **Pythagorean Theorem** — a² + b² = c²
- **Special Right Triangles** — 45-45-90 and 30-60-90 triangles
- **Trigonometric Ratios** — SOH-CAH-TOA for sine, cosine, tangent

## Core [[trigonometric-functions|Trigonometric Functions]]

### Basic Functions

- **Sine (sin)** — Opposite/Hypotenuse; oscillates between -1 and 1
- **Cosine (cos)** — Adjacent/Hypotenuse; oscillates between -1 and 1
- **Tangent (tan)** — Opposite/Adjacent; unbounded oscillation

### Reciprocal Functions

- **Cosecant (csc)** — 1/sin(θ)
- **Secant (sec)** — 1/cos(θ)
- **Cotangent (cot)** — 1/tan(θ) or Adjacent/Opposite

### Inverse Functions

- **Arcsin (sin⁻¹)** — Find angle given sine value
- **Arccos (cos⁻¹)** — Find angle given cosine value
- **Arctan (tan⁻¹)** — Find angle given tangent value

## Key Identities and Relationships

### Pythagorean Identities

- sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1
- 1 + tan²(θ) = sec²(θ)
- 1 + cot²(θ) = csc²(θ)

### Sum and Difference Formulas

- sin(A ± B) = sin(A)cos(B) ± cos(A)sin(B)
- cos(A ± B) = cos(A)cos(B) ∓ sin(A)sin(B)
- tan(A ± B) = (tan(A) ± tan(B))/(1 ∓ tan(A)tan(B))

### Double and Half Angle Formulas

- sin(2θ) = 2sin(θ)cos(θ)
- cos(2θ) = cos²(θ) - sin²(θ)
- tan(2θ) = 2tan(θ)/(1 - tan²(θ))

### Product and Sum-to-Product Formulas

- sin(A)sin(B) = ½[cos(A-B) - cos(A+B)]
- cos(A)cos(B) = ½[cos(A-B) + cos(A+B)]
- sin(A) + sin(B) = 2sin((A+B)/2)cos((A-B)/2)

## Trigonometric Equations

### Solving Equations

- **Standard form**: Isolate trig function, find angle, account for periodicity
- **General solution**: Include all equivalent angles (±2πn, ±360n°)
- **Multiple solutions**: Understand periodicity and symmetry

### Verification

- Use identities to simplify
- Check solutions in original equation
- Be aware of extraneous solutions from algebraic manipulation

---

## Graphs and Periodic Functions

### Function Behavior

- **Sine wave**: Starts at 0, oscillates with period 2π
- **Cosine wave**: Starts at 1, oscillates with period 2π
- **Tangent**: Periodic with period π, has vertical asymptotes
- **Amplitude, period, phase shift**: Parameters that transform graphs

### Applications to Waves

- Modeling oscillations and vibrations
- Harmonic motion and simple harmonic motion (SHM)
- Signal processing and Fourier analysis

## Non-Right Triangle Problems

### Law of Sines

- a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C)
- For finding unknown sides or angles

### Law of Cosines

- c² = a² + b² - 2ab·cos(C)
- For triangles where angle between two known sides is known

### Applications

- Navigation and surveying
- Oblique triangle solving
- Real-world geometry problems

## Common Problem Patterns

- **Finding unknown sides/angles**: Use SOH-CAH-TOA, Law of Sines, Law of Cosines

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting to account for multiple solutions due to periodicity
- Confusing radians and degrees
- Misapplying identities or formulas
- Incorrect calculator modes (degree vs radian)
- Not considering all possible angles in inverse function outputs

---

## Key Insights

- **Periodicity**: Trig functions repeat; solutions cycle every 2π (or π for tangent)
- **Unit circle**: Foundation for understanding all trig values and relationships
- **Symmetry**: Even/odd properties (cos is even, sin/tan are odd)
- **Complementary functions**: sin(θ) = cos(90° - θ)
- **Small angle approximation**: sin(θ) ≈ θ, cos(θ) ≈ 1 - θ²/2 for small θ in radians
