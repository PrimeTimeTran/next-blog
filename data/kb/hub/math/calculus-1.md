# 📘 Calculus 1 Hub

## 🧠 Purpose

Calculus 1 introduces the mathematics of **change and motion**.

It develops the tools to analyze:

- how quantities change (derivatives)
- how quantities accumulate (integrals)
- how limits define both ideas

---

## 🧠 Core Idea

> Calculus 1 is the study of **instantaneous change built from limiting processes**

Everything comes from limits:

- slopes of curves
- areas under curves
- continuity of functions

---

# 🧱 1. Limits & Continuity

> Limits define what happens near a point

- [[atom/math/calc/1/limits/limits|limits]] → behavior as x approaches a value
- [[continuity]] → no breaks in a function
- [[one-sided-limits|one-sided limits]]
- [[limits-at-infinity|limits at infinity]]
- algebraic techniques for limits

### Key intuition

A limit describes:

> what a function is approaching, not necessarily what it equals

- [[derivatives|Derivatives]] → slope of a function at a point

# 🧠 Core Mental Model

Calculus 1 is built on 3 ideas:

### 1. Limits

Define everything in calculus.

### 2. Local change

Derivatives describe instantaneous behavior.

### 3. Global accumulation

Integrals describe total effect.
