# Contacts / People — Reference Hub

## Purpose

A structured index of people you want to retain long-term memory about:

- professional connections
- mentors / peers
- collaborators
- recurring contacts worth tracking

This is not conceptual knowledge—it’s **relational data + context history**.

---

## Core Idea

Each person is a **node with evolving context**, not a static note.

You’re tracking:

- who they are
- how you know them
- what happened between you
- current relationship status

---

## Suggested Structure

### 1. People Index (Hub Level)

A single entry point:

- People / Contacts Hub
  - Work contacts
  - Friends
  - Mentors
  - Communities
  - Past colleagues

---

### 2. Individual Person Note (Template)

# [Name] — Contact Reference

## Who they are

Short identity snapshot (role, context, where you met them)

## Relationship context

How you know them + shared history

## Key interactions

- Project / event / collaboration
- Notable conversations or outcomes

## Skills / relevance

Why they matter in your network
(e.g. frontend engineer, hiring manager, startup founder)

## Last contact

Date + context

## Follow-ups

- Pending replies
- Things to revisit
- Opportunities

## Tags

- work
- friend
- recruiter
- engineer
- startup
- etc.

---

## Key Design Principle

### Treat people as “living records”

Not static info dumps.

They should evolve like:

- logs + memory
- not encyclopedia entries

---

## When NOT to use this

Avoid adding:

- random acquaintances with no relevance
- overly private/sensitive personal data
- short-term or one-off interactions
