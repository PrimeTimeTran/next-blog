# Computer Science (C.S.) Hub

### Purpose

Computer Science is the study of computation, information processing, and the systems that power modern digital technology, from software to hardware to intelligent systems.

---

### Phase 1: Foundations

Build the mathematical and physical foundations necessary for EE.

- [[algebra|Algebra]] and [[trigonometry|Trigonometry]] — For circuit analysis and signal processing
- [[calculus|Calculus]] — Limits, derivatives, integrals, multivariable calculus

## 1. Overview Map (RELATIONSHIP LAYER)

### Systems that store and move information

These systems focus on how data is represented, transmitted, and retrieved across environments rather than how it is computed.

Examples include databases and networking, where structure and communication are more important than logic execution.

---

### Systems that structure behavior

These systems define how software is organized, maintained, and extended over time.

They emphasize abstraction, modularity, and modeling real-world entities in code.

---

### Systems that solve computational problems

These systems focus on designing efficient procedures for solving structured problems under constraints.

They prioritize correctness, efficiency, and scalability of solutions.

---

### Systems that create user-facing products

These systems combine computation, structure, and interaction to build applications users directly engage with.

They emphasize usability, interfaces, and system integration.

---

### Systems that reason about intelligence

These systems focus on learning, adaptation, and decision-making from data.

They explore how machines can approximate reasoning, perception, and prediction.
