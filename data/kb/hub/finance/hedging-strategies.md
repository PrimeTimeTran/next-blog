# 🧠 Hedging Strategies Hub

Hedging is a risk management approach used by investors to **reduce exposure to unwanted risk** while maintaining desired positions in the market.

Instead of maximizing profit, hedging focuses on:

> controlling downside risk and stabilizing returns

---

# 🧭 Core Idea of Hedging

A hedge works by taking an **offsetting position** against an existing exposure.

:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Goal:

> Reduce variance of returns, not eliminate all risk

---

# 📊 Why Hedging Is Used

- Protect against downside risk
- Reduce portfolio volatility
- Lock in profits
- Manage uncertainty in volatile markets
- Stabilize cash flows for institutions

---

# 🧱 Core Types of Hedging Strategies

## 1. [[pair-trading|Pair Trading]]

Market-neutral strategy using two correlated assets.

- Long undervalued asset
- Short overvalued asset
- Profits from convergence

---

## 2. [[Options Hedging]]

Using derivatives to limit downside risk.

### Key instruments:

- Put options (insurance against price drops)
- Call options (hedging short exposure)
- Protective puts
- Covered calls

:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## 3. [[Delta Hedging]]

Used in options pricing to neutralize price sensitivity.

- Adjust positions to keep delta ≈ 0
- Common in market-making and derivatives desks

---

## 4. [[Futures Hedging]]

Locking in future prices using futures contracts.

- Farmers hedging crop prices
- Airlines hedging fuel costs
- Investors hedging index exposure

---

## 5. [[Diversification]]

Hedging through portfolio construction.

- Spread capital across uncorrelated assets
- Reduces unsystematic risk

:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## 6. [[Short Selling as Hedge]]

Using short positions to offset long exposure.

- Hedge individual stock risk
- Hedge sector exposure
- Hedge beta exposure

---

## 7. [[Statistical Arbitrage]]

Model-driven hedging using statistical relationships.

- Mean reversion strategies
- Cointegration-based trades
- Multi-asset hedging baskets

---

## 8. [[Currency Hedging]]

Used in international investing.

- FX forwards
- Currency swaps
- Hedged ETFs

---

# 🧠 Key Hedging Dimensions

## Exposure Types

- Market risk (systematic)
- Asset-specific risk (idiosyncratic)
- Interest rate risk
- Currency risk

---

## Hedge Effectiveness

A hedge is effective when it:

- reduces variance
- preserves upside potential
- is cost-efficient

---

# ⚖️ Trade-Offs

Hedging is not free:

- Cost of derivatives
- Reduced upside profit
- Execution complexity
- Model risk

---

# 🧠 Big Picture Mental Model

Hedging =

> “Pay a cost today to reduce uncertainty tomorrow”

---

# 📌 Summary

Hedging strategies are tools that combine derivatives, portfolio construction, and statistical relationships to reduce financial risk while maintaining exposure to desired returns.
