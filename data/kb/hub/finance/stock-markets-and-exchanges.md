# Stock Markets & Exchanges Hub

## Definition

Stock markets and exchanges are **organized platforms where securities (stocks, bonds, derivatives) are traded**, connecting buyers and sellers to facilitate capital allocation, price discovery, and liquidity provision in the economy.

---

## Core Functions of Stock Markets

### 1. Price Discovery

> Determining fair market prices through supply and demand

- Auction mechanism
- Information aggregation
- Efficient pricing of risk and return

**Core idea:** Markets reveal what assets are truly worth through collective trading activity.

---

### 2. Liquidity Provision

> Making it easy to buy and sell assets quickly

- Bid-ask spreads
- Trading volume
- Market depth
- Transaction costs

**Core idea:** Markets transform illiquid investments into liquid assets.

---

### 3. Capital Allocation

> Directing savings to productive investments

- IPOs and secondary offerings
- Venture capital to public markets
- Corporate financing
- Economic growth facilitation

**Core idea:** Markets connect savers with borrowers efficiently.

---

### 4. Risk Management

> Tools for hedging and diversification

- Derivatives markets
- Options and futures
- Portfolio insurance
- Risk transfer mechanisms

**Core idea:** Markets provide mechanisms to manage uncertainty.

---

## Market Structure & Participants

### Primary Participants

- **Retail investors** — individual traders
- **Institutional investors** — pension funds, mutual funds, hedge funds
- **Market makers** — provide liquidity, quote bid/ask spreads
- **Specialists** — maintain orderly markets in specific stocks
- **High-frequency traders** — algorithmic trading firms

### Supporting Infrastructure

- **Clearing houses** — guarantee trade settlement
- **Custodians** — hold securities safely
- **Transfer agents** — handle stock registration
- **Depositories** — electronic book-entry systems

---

## Trading Mechanisms

### Order Types

- **Market orders** — execute immediately at best available price
- **Limit orders** — execute only at specified price or better
- **Stop orders** — become market orders when price threshold reached
- **Stop-limit orders** — become limit orders at stop price

### Auction Types

- **Continuous trading** — throughout trading day
- **Opening/closing auctions** — single price determination
- **Call auctions** — periodic batch trading

### Matching Algorithms

- [[order-books|Order Books]]
- **Price-time priority**
- **Pro-rata matching**
- **Volume-weighted average price (VWAP)**

## Market Regulation & Oversight

### Regulatory Bodies

- **SEC (US)** — Securities and Exchange Commission
- **FINRA** — Financial Industry Regulatory Authority
- **ESMA (EU)** — European Securities and Markets Authority
- **IOSCO** — International Organization of Securities Commissions

### Key Regulations

- **Securities Act of 1933** — disclosure requirements for new issues
- **Securities Exchange Act of 1934** — ongoing reporting and market oversight
- **Sarbanes-Oxley Act** — corporate governance and accounting standards
- **Dodd-Frank Act** — post-crisis financial reforms

### Market Surveillance

- **Trade monitoring** — detect manipulation and insider trading
- **Circuit breakers** — halt trading during extreme volatility
- **Position limits** — prevent excessive speculation

## Market Efficiency

### Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)

- **Weak form** — past prices don't predict future prices
- **Semi-strong form** — all public information reflected in prices
- **Strong form** — all information (public and private) reflected in prices

### Market Anomalies

- **January effect** — stocks rise in January
- **Momentum effect** — winning stocks keep winning
- **Value effect** — cheap stocks outperform expensive ones
- **Size effect** — small companies outperform large ones

## Market Microstructure

### Bid-Ask Spread

- **Quoted spread** — difference between best bid and ask
- **Effective spread** — actual trading costs
- **Realized spread** — market maker profit

### Order Flow & Liquidity

- **Order book dynamics**
- **Market impact** — how trades affect prices
- **Slippage** — difference between expected and actual execution price

### Transaction Costs

- **Explicit costs** — commissions, fees
- **Implicit costs** — bid-ask spread, market impact
- **Information asymmetry costs**
