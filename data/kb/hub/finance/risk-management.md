# 🛡️ Risk Management Hub

Risk management is the discipline of **identifying, measuring, and controlling financial uncertainty** to reduce downside risk while preserving return potential.

---

# 🧠 Core Idea

All financial systems contain uncertainty.

Risk management does not eliminate risk — it:

> structures, quantifies, and constrains it

---

# 🧭 1. Risk Identification (What can go wrong?)

## 🟦 Market Risk

Price movement risk:

- equities
- interest rates
- FX
- commodities

---

## 🟩 Credit Risk

Counterparty default risk:

- defaults
- credit downgrade
- spread widening

---

## 🟨 Liquidity Risk

Inability to trade efficiently:

- thin markets
- wide spreads
- forced liquidation

---

## 🟥 Operational Risk

Internal system failures:

- human error
- fraud
- system outages

---

## 🟪 Systemic Risk

System-wide breakdown risk:

- financial crises
- contagion effects
- banking collapse

# 🧮 3. Quantitative Risk Management (ENGINE LAYER)

This is the mathematical system that powers modern risk management.

## 🧠 What quants do

- Build risk models
- Simulate market outcomes
- Estimate tail risk
- Allocate capital based on risk
- Stress-test portfolios

---

## 🔧 Core tools

### Portfolio variance

:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

---

### Monte Carlo simulation

- simulate thousands of price paths
- estimate probability of extreme losses

---

### Stress testing

- market crashes
- liquidity freezes
- correlation breakdowns

---

## 🧠 Key insight

> Risk models estimate probability — they do not guarantee safety

# 🧱 4. Risk Control (What we do about it)

## [[Diversification]]

Reduce exposure across uncorrelated assets

---

## [[Hedging]]

Offset risk using opposing positions:

- options
- futures
- short selling
- pair trading

---

## [[Position Sizing]]

Control exposure per trade or asset

---

## [[Tail Protection]]

Protect against extreme events:

- put options
- volatility hedges

---

## [[Stop Loss / Risk Limits]]

Predefined exit rules to cap losses

---

## [[Scenario Analysis]]

Test performance under hypothetical shocks

---

## [[Correlation Risk Management]]

Manage hidden dependency between assets

# 🏗️ 5. Risk System Architecture (Mental Model)

A complete risk system has 3 layers:

## 1. Identification

What can go wrong?

## 2. Measurement (Quant layer)

How likely and how large?

## 3. Control

How do we reduce or survive it?
