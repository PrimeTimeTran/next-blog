# Market Efficiency Hub

## Definition

Market efficiency refers to **how well financial markets incorporate and reflect all available information in asset prices**. Efficient markets quickly and accurately price securities based on fundamental value, making it impossible to consistently achieve abnormal returns through information analysis.

---

## Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)

### Three Forms of Market Efficiency

#### Weak Form Efficiency

> Past price information cannot predict future prices

- **Random walk theory** — price changes are unpredictable
- **Technical analysis** — chart patterns, momentum indicators
- **Evidence** — autocorrelation tests, runs tests

**Core idea:** Historical price data is fully reflected in current prices.

---

#### Semi-Strong Form Efficiency

> All publicly available information is reflected in prices

- **Fundamental analysis** — financial statements, economic data
- **Event studies** — price reactions to news announcements
- **Information processing** — speed and accuracy of price adjustment

**Core idea:** Markets rapidly incorporate all public information.

---

#### Strong Form Efficiency

> All information (public and private) is reflected in prices

- **Insider trading** — private information advantage
- **Professional analysis** — expert research and analysis
- **Institutional trading** — large investor information advantages

**Core idea:** Even insiders cannot consistently earn abnormal returns.

---

## Random Walk Theory

### Price Movement Characteristics

- **Independent increments** — each price change is independent
- **Identically distributed** — price changes follow same distribution
- **Martingale property** — future prices unpredictable from past

### Statistical Tests

- **Serial correlation** — absence of predictable patterns
- **Runs tests** — equal probability of up/down movements
- **Variance ratio tests** — check for mean reversion

---

## Market Anomalies

### Calendar Anomalies

- **January effect** — stocks rise in January
- **Day-of-the-week effect** — Monday returns lower
- **Holiday effect** — pre-holiday price increases
- **Turn-of-the-month effect** — higher returns at month end

### Fundamental Anomalies

- **Size effect** — small stocks outperform large stocks
- **Value effect** — value stocks (low P/B, P/E) outperform growth
- **Momentum effect** — past winners continue to win
- **Post-earnings drift** — earnings surprise persistence

### Behavioral Anomalies

- **Disposition effect** — investors hold losers, sell winners
- **Overconfidence** — investors overestimate abilities
- **Herding** — investors follow crowd behavior
- **Anchoring** — prices stick to reference points

---

## Behavioral Finance Challenges

### Cognitive Biases

- **Loss aversion** — losses hurt more than equivalent gains
- **Confirmation bias** — seek information supporting beliefs
- **Availability heuristic** — judge probability by memorable events
- **Representativeness** — generalize from small samples

### Limits to Arbitrage

- **Fundamental risk** — arbitrage positions can lose money
- **Noise trader risk** — irrational traders can influence prices
- **Implementation costs** — transaction costs, short-selling constraints
- **Model risk** — mispricing may persist longer than expected

---

## Empirical Evidence

### Supporting EMH

- **Event study methodology** — prices adjust quickly to news
- **Mutual fund performance** — few consistently beat market
- **Random walk evidence** — technical analysis fails long-term
- **Grossman-Stiglitz paradox** — information acquisition incentives

### Challenging EMH

- **Anomaly persistence** — value and momentum effects endure
- **Limits to arbitrage** — rational traders can't always correct mispricing
- **Behavioral evidence** — psychological factors influence prices
- **Market bubbles** — irrational exuberance and crashes

---

## Adaptive Markets Hypothesis

### Core Ideas

- **Evolutionary perspective** — markets adapt to changing environments
- **Survival of the fittest** — successful strategies persist
- **Changing efficiency** — markets become more/less efficient over time
- **Individual differences** — investors have different skills and information

### Implications

- **Dynamic efficiency** — efficiency varies across markets and time
- **Behavioral foundations** — evolution explains market phenomena
- **Practical investing** — adapt strategies to market conditions

## Implications for Investors

### Active vs Passive Investing

- **Active management** — attempt to beat market (difficult in efficient markets)
- **Passive indexing** — track market returns (appropriate for efficient markets)
- **Market timing** — predict market movements (generally unsuccessful)
- **Security selection** — pick undervalued stocks (challenging)

### Investment Strategies

- **Value investing** — buy cheap assets (exploits value anomaly)
- **Momentum investing** — follow trends (exploits momentum anomaly)
- **Factor investing** — systematic factor exposure
- **Arbitrage strategies** — exploit price differences

## Criticisms & Extensions

### EMH Limitations

- **Transaction costs** — ignored in theoretical models
- **Taxes** — affect investment decisions
- **Liquidity constraints** — inability to trade freely
- **Short-selling restrictions** — limit arbitrage

### Alternative Theories

- **Noise trader theory** — irrational traders influence prices
- **Fractal markets** — self-similar patterns at different scales
- **Complexity theory** — markets as complex adaptive systems
- **Quantum finance** — uncertainty and observer effects

## Testing Market Efficiency

### Statistical Methods

- **Unit root tests** — check for random walk properties
- **Cointegration tests** — long-run relationships
- **Granger causality** — predictive relationships
- **Bootstrap methods** — statistical significance testing

### Event Study Methodology

- **Abnormal returns** — actual vs expected performance
- **Cumulative abnormal returns (CAR)** — summed effects
- **Market model** — risk-adjusted expected returns
- **Statistical significance** — t-tests, confidence intervals
