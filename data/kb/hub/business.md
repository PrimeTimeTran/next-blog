# Business Administration Hub

**Definition**
Business administration is the study of how organizations are planned, operated, and controlled to achieve goals efficiently and sustainably. It integrates management, finance, marketing, operations, and strategy into a unified system for decision-making.

---

## Core Pillars of Business

### 1. Management & Organization

> How work and people are structured and coordinated

- Management functions (planning, organizing, leading, controlling)
- Organizational structures (hierarchical, matrix, flat)
- Leadership styles
- Organizational behavior (motivation, culture, decision-making)

---

### 2. Accounting & Finance

> How money flows through a business

- Financial statements (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
- Budgeting and forecasting
- Cost analysis (fixed vs variable costs)
- Time value of money
- Investment decisions (NPV, ROI basics)
- Capital structure (debt vs equity)

---

### 3. Marketing

> How value is created and delivered to customers

- Market research & consumer behavior
- Segmentation, targeting, positioning (STP)
- Marketing mix ([[4-Ps|4Ps: Product, Price, Place, Promotion]])
- Branding and positioning
- Digital marketing fundamentals

---

### 4. Operations Management

> How goods and services are produced efficiently

- Process design and optimization
- Supply chain management
- Inventory systems
- Quality control (Six Sigma basics)
- Capacity planning and logistics

---

### 5. Economics for Business

> How markets and incentives shape decisions

- Microeconomics (supply, demand, elasticity)
- Market structures (monopoly, oligopoly, competition)
- Macroeconomics (GDP, inflation, interest rates)
- Business cycles
- Policy impact on firms

---

### 6. Strategy

> How businesses compete and win long-term

- Competitive advantage
- Porter’s Five Forces
- SWOT analysis
- Business models
- Corporate strategy vs business unit strategy
- Scaling and growth strategy

---

### 7. Business Law & Ethics

> Rules and responsibilities of operating a business

- Contracts and obligations
- Corporate governance
- Employment law basics
- Intellectual property
- Ethical decision-making frameworks
- Compliance and regulation

---

### 8. Entrepreneurship

> Creating and growing new ventures

- Opportunity identification
- Business model design
- Startup funding (bootstrapping, VC, angel)
- Lean startup methodology
- Risk and uncertainty management

---

### 9. Information Systems & Business Tech

> How technology supports business operations

- Enterprise systems (ERP, CRM)
- Data-driven decision making
- Business analytics
- Digital transformation
- Automation and AI in business

---

## Cross-Cutting Skills

These apply across all domains:

- Decision-making under uncertainty
- Financial literacy
- Communication & negotiation
- Data interpretation
- Problem structuring
- Systems thinking

---

## Mental Model of Business

> A business is a **value transformation system**

Inputs:

- Capital
- Labor
- Information

Process:

- Operations + management + strategy

Outputs:

- Products/services
- Revenue
- Customer value

---

## How the domains connect

- Finance = resource constraint layer
- Marketing = demand creation layer
- Operations = delivery layer
- Strategy = direction layer
- Management = coordination layer
- Economics = external environment layer

---

## One-Line Intuition

> Business administration is the study of how to **design, run, and scale systems that convert resources into value under constraints**
