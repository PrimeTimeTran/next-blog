# Chart vs. Graph: Understanding the Graphical Representation

A diagram is a specific, structured, and symbolic representation (like a flowchart or graph) designed to explain processes, relationships, or systems. A "visual" is a broader term covering any graphical item—photos, illustrations, or diagrams—meant to convey information or enhance appeal. Diagrams focus on technical structure; visuals can be purely aesthetic or informational.

### Key Differences:

- Definition: A diagram is a structured, often abstract schematic (e.g., UML diagram, flowchart). A "visual" is any visual aid, including photos, videos, or diagrams.
- Purpose: Diagrams explain how things work, their relationships, or their structure. Visuals can educate, persuade, or simply grab attention.
- Components: Diagrams use lines, shapes, and text to show relationships. Visuals include a wider variety of imagery, such as photos or graphs.
- Types: Diagrams include Venn diagrams, flowcharts, and technical drawings. Visuals include infographics, photographs, and charts.

### When to Use Which:

- Use a Diagram: To explain complex processes, relationships, or systems, such as a flowchart or a technical schematic.
- Use a Visual: To improve engagement, convey emotion, or provide context, such as a photo or infographic.

Both can be used together to make complex information easier to understand.
