# 📊 Startup Scorecard Grading Rubric

> Use this to assign **consistent 0–10 scores** across all categories.

---

# 🧠 Universal Scale (applies to ALL categories)

## 0–2 → 🚫 Very Weak

- No clear evidence
- Vague answers
- Pure idea / speculation
- Major red flags

👉 Example:

- “We think people might want this”

---

## 3–4 → ⚠️ Weak

- Some thought, little proof
- Early idea, minimal validation
- Lots of assumptions

👉 Example:

- Small user tests, no real traction

---

## 5–6 → ⚖️ متوسط (Average)

- Reasonable but unproven
- Some signals of correctness
- Still risky / unclear

👉 Example:

- Early users OR early revenue, but not both

---

## 7–8 → ✅ Strong

- Clear validation
- Evidence-backed claims
- Momentum visible

👉 Example:

- Growing users, working product, clear direction

---

## 9–10 → 🚀 Exceptional

- Strong proof + execution
- Clear advantage
- Hard to compete with

👉 Example:

- Rapid growth, strong team, obvious demand

---

# 📦 Category-Specific Anchors

## 1. Problem & Market

- 0–2: Problem unclear or fake
- 5–6: Real problem, unclear demand
- 9–10: Painful problem + proven demand

---

## 📈 2. Product & PMF

- 0–2: No product / idea only
- 5–6: MVP with some usage
- 9–10: Strong traction (users + retention + growth)

---

## 📈 3. Growth & Distribution

- 0–2: No acquisition strategy
- 5–6: Some channels tested
- 9–10: Repeatable + scalable growth engine

---

## 💰 4. Business Model

- 0–2: No clear monetization
- 5–6: Plausible but unproven
- 9–10: Proven + scalable revenue model

---

## 🧾 5. Financials & Fundraising

- 0–2: No runway / unclear funding
- 5–6: Some runway, unclear future
- 9–10: Strong backing + clear plan

---

## 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 6. Team & Founders

- 0–2: Weak / mismatched / unclear
- 5–6: Competent but unproven
- 9–10: Proven builders with domain expertise

---

## ⚖️ 7. Role & Personal Fit

- 0–2: Vague role, low ownership
- 5–6: Defined role, moderate upside
- 9–10: High ownership + strong upside + aligned

---

## ⚠️ 8. Risk Profile

_(Reverse scoring: higher = safer)_

- 0–2: Extremely fragile (many unknowns)
- 5–6: Moderate risk
- 9–10: Well-understood, manageable risk

---

# 🚨 Consistency Rules (IMPORTANT)

## Rule 1: Evidence > Words

> Never score based on confidence or charisma.

Score based on:

- data
- traction
- proof

---

## Rule 2: Default to Lower Score

> If unsure, round DOWN

This prevents optimism bias.

---

## Rule 3: One Strong Signal ≠ High Score

Example:

- good idea BUT no users → still low score

---

## Rule 4: Penalize Vagueness

If answers are:

- unclear
- hand-wavy
- “we’ll figure it out”

→ cap score at **≤ 4**

---

# 🧠 Meta Insight

You are grading:

> **Reality, not potential**

Most founders sell potential.

You score:

- execution
- evidence
- clarity

---

# 📌 Final Rule

> A startup is only as strong as its weakest critical category.

Example:

- Great team + no market → still bad
- Great idea + no distribution → still bad
