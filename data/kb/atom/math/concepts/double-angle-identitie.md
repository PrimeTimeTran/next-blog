### 🧠 Atom: Double-Angle Identities (Trigonometry)

**Double-angle identities** are formulas that express trigonometric functions of (2\theta) in terms of functions of (\theta). They are used to simplify expressions and rewrite higher-angle trig functions using lower-angle ones.

---

## 📌 Core identities

### Sine double-angle

$$
\sin(2\theta)=2\sin\theta\cos\theta
$$

---

### Cosine double-angle (three equivalent forms)

$$
cos(2\theta)=\cos^2\theta-\sin^2\theta
$$

Using identities:

$\cos(2\theta)=2\cos^2\theta-1$
$\cos(2\theta)=1-2\sin^2\theta$

---

### Tangent double-angle

$\tan(2\theta)=\frac{2\tan\theta}{1-\tan^2\theta$

---

## 🧠 Intuition

Double-angle identities let you:

- replace “double angles” with simpler expressions
- reduce trig powers or complexity
- rewrite expressions into a solvable form

They come directly from **angle addition formulas**:
$\sin(a+b), \quad \cos(a+b), \quad \tan(a+b)$

by setting $a = b = \theta$.

---

## 🔍 When they are used

- Simplifying trig expressions
- Solving equations involving $\sin(2x), \cos(2x), \tan(2x)$
- Integration (especially trig integrals)
- Converting powers of trig functions into linear forms

---

## ⚠️ Common confusion

- There is **not one cosine identity** — there are **three equivalent forms**
- You choose the form depending on whether you want:
  - everything in terms of $\sin(2x), \cos(2x), \tan(2x)$
  - everything in terms of $\cos$
  - or a mixed expression

---

## 🧠 Mental model

> Double-angle identities are “rewriting rules” that reduce angle complexity at the cost of algebraic expansion.
