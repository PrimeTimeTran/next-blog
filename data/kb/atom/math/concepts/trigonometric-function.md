# 📌 Trigonometric Functions

## 🧠 Core Idea

Trigonometric functions describe **fixed ratios between the sides of a right triangle based on an angle**.

They convert:

> **Angle → geometric ratio**

---

## 📐 Definition (Right Triangle)

For an angle $\theta$:

$$
\sin(\theta)=\frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}
$$

$$
\cos(\theta)=\frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}
$$

$$
\tan(\theta)=\frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}
$$

---

## 🔑 Key Insight

For a fixed angle:

- All right triangles with that angle are **similar**
- So their side ratios are **constant**

👉 Trig functions encode those constants.

---

## 🧭 Interpretation

- **sin(θ)** → vertical proportion (height vs hypotenuse)
- **cos(θ)** → horizontal proportion (base vs hypotenuse)
- **tan(θ)** → slope (rise over run)

---

## 🧩 Primary Use Cases

- Finding unknown sides in right triangles
- Breaking vectors into components
- Modeling periodic behavior (waves, cycles)
- Physics: motion, forces, oscillations
- Graphics: rotations and projections

---

## 🧠 One-line Memory Hook

> Trig functions turn an angle into **stable geometric ratios that let you compute shape and motion**.
