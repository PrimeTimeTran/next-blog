## Axiom

### Definition

An **axiom** is a statement accepted as true **without proof**, used as a starting point for building a logical system.

---

### Role in mathematics

- Serves as a **foundation** for all reasoning in a system
- Defines the “rules of the game” for a mathematical structure
- Everything else is derived from axioms using logic

---

### Key properties

- Not proven within the system
- Chosen for **consistency and usefulness**
- Must not contradict other axioms
- Can vary between mathematical systems

---

### Examples

- Euclidean geometry:

  - “Through any two points, there is exactly one straight line.”

- Real numbers:

  - Field axioms (associativity, distributivity, identity elements)

---

### Intuition

Think of axioms as:

> the ground rules that define what kind of mathematical world you are working in

---

# Related concepts

- Theorem (proven from axioms)
- Definition (describes objects, not truths)
- Formal system (collection of axioms + rules of logic)
