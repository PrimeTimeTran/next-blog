## Theorem

### Definition

A **theorem** is a mathematical statement that has been **proven true** using axioms, definitions, and previously established results.

---

### Role in mathematics

- Acts as a **confirmed result** within a logical system
- Builds the structure of mathematical theory
- Serves as a foundation for further results (lemmas, corollaries, etc.)

---

### Structure

A theorem typically has:

1. **Statement**
   What is claimed to be true

2. **Assumptions (hypotheses)**
   Conditions under which the statement holds

3. **Proof**
   A logically valid argument establishing truth

---

### Types of related results

- **Lemma**: helper theorem used to prove larger results
- **Corollary**: direct consequence of a theorem
- **Proposition**: smaller or less central theorem (usage varies)

---

### Example idea

If ( n ) is even, then ( n^2 ) is even.

This becomes a theorem once a valid proof is provided.

---

### Intuition

A theorem is:

> a statement that survives rigorous logical testing

---

### Common characteristics

- Depends on axioms and earlier results
- Cannot be accepted without proof
- Becomes part of the permanent mathematical framework once proven

---

# Related concepts

- Axiom (assumed true without proof)
- Proof (logical justification)
- Lemma / Corollary (supporting or derived results)
