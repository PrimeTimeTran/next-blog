# 🧠 Statistics

**Statistics** is the field concerned with **collecting, organizing, analyzing, and interpreting data** to understand patterns, make decisions, and quantify uncertainty.

---

## 📌 Core idea

Statistics turns data into information:

$$
\text{Data} \rightarrow \text{Summary} \rightarrow \text{Inference} \rightarrow \text{Decision}
$$

It provides tools to answer questions like:

- What is typical in this data?
- What is uncertain or variable?
- What can we infer about a larger population?

---

## 🧱 Two main branches

### 1. Descriptive statistics

Focus: **summarizing data**

- Mean, median, mode
- Variance, standard deviation
- Visualizations (histograms, box plots)

👉 Goal: describe what you see

---

### 2. Inferential statistics

Focus: **making conclusions from samples**

- Estimation (e.g., confidence intervals)
- Hypothesis testing
- Regression models

👉 Goal: infer what you _don’t directly observe_

---

## 🎲 Key concept: Uncertainty

Statistics fundamentally deals with:

> variability and randomness in data

It does not assume perfect knowledge — it quantifies uncertainty instead.

---

## 📊 Example

Suppose you measure exam scores of 100 students:

- Descriptive: average score = 72
- Inferential: estimate average score of all students in the school

---

## 🧠 Connection to probability

- Probability: models randomness _before seeing data_
- Statistics: learns from randomness _after seeing data_

They are inverse perspectives of uncertainty.

---

## ⚠️ Common misunderstanding

Statistics is not just “calculating averages.”

It is:

> a framework for reasoning under uncertainty using data

---

## 🔗 Related concepts

- Probability theory (foundation)
- Random variables (modeling data)
- Estimation theory (learning parameters)
- Hypothesis testing (decision-making)
- Regression (relationships between variables)
