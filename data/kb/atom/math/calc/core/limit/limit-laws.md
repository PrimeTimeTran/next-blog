# 📘 Limit Laws

Limit laws are rules that allow you to **break complex limits into simpler parts**.

---

# 🧠 Core Idea

> If limits exist, you can compute them piece by piece.

---

# 📊 Basic Limit Laws

Assume:

$$
\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L
\quad \text{and} \quad
\lim_{x \to a} g(x) = M
$$

---

## ➕ Sum Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} (f(x) + g(x)) = L + M
$$

---

## ➖ Difference Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} (f(x) - g(x)) = L - M
$$

---

## ✖️ Product Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} (f(x) \cdot g(x)) = LM
$$

---

## ➗ Quotient Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{L}{M}, \quad M \ne 0
$$

---

## 🔢 Constant Multiple Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} (c \cdot f(x)) = cL
$$

---

## 📦 Power Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} (f(x))^n = L^n
$$

---

## 📏 Root Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} \sqrt[n]{f(x)} = \sqrt[n]{L}
$$

(when defined)

---

## 🧩 Identity Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} x = a
$$

---

## 🔢 Constant Law

$$
\lim_{x \to a} c = c
$$

---

# ⚠️ Key Condition

These laws only work if:

> the individual limits exist

Otherwise, you cannot freely apply them.

---

# 🧠 Key Insight

Limit laws let you:

> reduce complicated expressions into smaller limit pieces

They are the algebra rules of limits.

---

# 📈 Why they matter

They are used in:

- evaluating limits directly
- simplifying indeterminate forms (after factoring)
- proving continuity
- building derivatives and integrals

---

# 🧭 Mental Model

> Limits behave like algebra — but only when the pieces behave nicely.

---

# 📌 Summary

- Break limits into parts
- Apply algebraic rules (sum, product, quotient, etc.)
- Requires existence of individual limits
- Foundation for all limit evaluation techniques
