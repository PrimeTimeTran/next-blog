# 📌 Atom Note: Critical Points

## 🧩 Core Idea

A **critical point** is a point on a function where the function’s slope is zero or undefined. These points are where the function may change behavior (e.g., local max, local min, or neither).

---

## 📐 Definition

A point $x = c$ is a **critical point** of $f(x)$ if:

- $f'(c) = 0$, or
- $f'(c)$ is undefined (but $f(c)$ exists)

---

## 🔍 Why they matter

Critical points are where:

- the function may **stop increasing and start decreasing**, or vice versa
- local extrema (max/min) can occur
- the graph may have sharp turns or cusps

---

## 📊 Types of behavior at critical points

A critical point can be:

### 📈 Local maximum

Function goes up then down

### 📉 Local minimum

Function goes down then up

### ➖ Neither

No change in increasing/decreasing behavior

---

## 🧠 Intuition

Think of a graph as motion:

- $f'(x) > 0$ → moving upward
- $f'(x) < 0$ → moving downward

A critical point is where:

> “motion pauses or becomes undefined”

But what happens next determines whether it’s a peak, valley, or flat pass-through.
