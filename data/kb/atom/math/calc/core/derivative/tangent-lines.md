# 📘 Tangent Lines

A tangent line is a straight line that touches a curve at a point and matches its instantaneous direction at that point.

---

# 🧠 Core Idea

> A tangent line is the best linear approximation of a function at a single point.

---

# 📊 Definition

For a function $f(x)$ at a point $x = a$:

- point on curve:

  $$
  (a, f(a))
  $$

- slope of tangent line:
  $$
  f'(a)
  $$

---

# 📈 Equation of Tangent Line

Using point-slope form:

$$
y - f(a) = f'(a)(x - a)
$$

---

# 🔍 Meaning

A tangent line:

- touches the curve at exactly one point (locally)
- has the same slope as the function at that point
- approximates the function near $a$

---

# 🧠 Key Insight

> The derivative gives the slope of the tangent line.

So:

- function → curve
- derivative → slope
- tangent line → linear approximation

---

# 📊 Geometric Interpretation

A tangent line is the limit of secant lines:

$$
f'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}
$$

---

# 📈 Why it matters

Tangent lines are used for:

- local approximation (linearization)
- physics (instantaneous velocity)
- optimization (slope analysis)
- curve behavior analysis

---

# ⚠️ Common Misconception

A tangent line does NOT mean:

- “it only touches once globally”

Instead:

> it matches the curve’s direction at a point (local property)

---

# 🔗 Connection to Derivatives

- derivative = slope function
- tangent line = geometric interpretation of derivative

---

# 🧭 Mental Model

> Zoom in enough on a curve, and it looks like a straight line — that line is the tangent.

---

# 📌 Summary

- Tangent line = line with slope $f'(a)$ at point $a$
- Equation: $y - f(a) = f'(a)(x - a)$
- Represents instantaneous direction of a function
- Fundamental link between geometry and derivatives
