## 🟦 Rates of Change (Calculus Big Picture)

- Calculus studies how quantities _change_, not just their values
- A **rate of change** compares how one quantity changes relative to another:
  $$
  \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}
  $$

---

**Average vs Instantaneous Change**

- Average rate of change (over an interval):
  $$
  \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}
  $$
- Instantaneous rate of change (at a point):
  $$
  \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a + h) - f(a)}{h}
  $$

---

**Core Idea**

> The derivative measures how a function is changing _at an exact moment_.

---

**Geometric Meaning**

- Rate of change = slope of the tangent line
- Approximates the function locally as linear

---

**Key Insight**

- Zooming in on a curve makes it look like a straight line
- The slope of that line is the instantaneous rate of change

---

**Interpretations**

- Physics: velocity = rate of change of position
- Economics: marginal change
- General: sensitivity of output to input

---

**Mental Model**

> A derivative tells you how fast something is changing _right now_, and in what direction.
