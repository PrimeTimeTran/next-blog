# 🧩 Sum Rule (Derivative Atom)

## Core idea

The derivative of a sum is the sum of the derivatives.

---

## Rule

$$
\frac{d}{dx}[f(x) + g(x)] = f'(x) + g'(x)
$$

---

## Meaning

Differentiation acts on each term independently—there is no interaction between added parts.

---

## Intuition

A function made of multiple pieces changes according to how **each piece changes on its own**.

So:

- each term contributes its own rate of change
- those rates just add together

---

## Example

$$
\frac{d}{dx}(x^2 + 3x + 5)
$$

Break it apart:

- $\frac{d}{dx}(x^2) = 2x$
- $\frac{d}{dx}(3x) = 3$
- $\frac{d}{dx}(5) = 0$

So:

$$
	\frac{d}{dx}(x^2 + 3x + 5) = 2x + 3
$$

---

## Geometric view

- Each term shapes the curve
- The derivative combines how each shape contributes to the slope at every point

---

## Key takeaway

> The derivative distributes across addition: each part changes independently, and their rates simply add.
