# 🧩 Constant Multiple Rule (Derivative Atom)

## Core idea

A constant factor stays unchanged when taking a derivative.

---

## Rule

$$
\frac{d}{dx}[c \cdot f(x)] = c \cdot f'(x)
$$

---

## Meaning

If a function is scaled by a constant, its rate of change is scaled by the same constant.

---

## Intuition

The constant (c) does not depend on (x), so it does not “change during differentiation.”

Only the function part changes; the scaling just carries through.

---

## Example

$$
\frac{d}{dx}[3x^2]
$$

Step:

- Derivative of $x^2$ is $2x$
- Keep the 3

Result:

$$
= 3 \cdot 2x = 6x
$$

---

## Geometric view

Multiplying a function by a constant stretches or compresses it vertically.

- The shape scales
- The slope scales the same way

So the derivative scales too.

---

## Key takeaway

> Constants act like fixed scaling factors—they pass through differentiation unchanged.
