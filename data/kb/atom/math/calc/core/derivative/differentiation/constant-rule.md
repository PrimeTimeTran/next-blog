# 🧩 Constant Rule

## Core idea

The derivative of a constant is zero.

$\frac{d}{dx}[c] = 0$

---

## Meaning

A constant does not change as $x$ changes, so its rate of change is zero everywhere.

---

## Geometric interpretation

- $y = c$ is a horizontal line
- Horizontal lines have slope $0$
- Therefore, the tangent slope is always $0$

---

## Intuition

No matter how much (x) moves:

- the output stays the same
- so there is no increase or decrease

---

## Key takeaway

> Constants carry no variation, so they contribute nothing to a derivative.
