# 🧩 Power Rule (Derivative Atom)

## Core idea

To differentiate a power of (x), bring down the exponent and reduce it by 1.

---

## Rule

$$
\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = n x^{n-1}
$$

---

## Meaning

Each power of (x) describes how fast growth scales, and the derivative adjusts both:

- the **rate (multiply by (n))**
- the **shape (reduce power by 1)**

---

## Examples

- $\frac{d}{dx}[x^2] = 2x$
- $\frac{d}{dx}[x^3] = 3x^2$
- $\frac{d}{dx}[x] = 1$
- $\frac{d}{dx}[1] = 0$ (fits constant rule)

---

## Intuition

Higher powers grow faster, so:

- the exponent becomes a scaling factor
- and the function “drops a degree” of growth

---

## Geometric view

- $x^n$ shapes curvature
- derivative measures how steep that shape is at each point

---

## Key takeaway

> Differentiation of powers reduces complexity: “bring the exponent down, then reduce it by one power.”
