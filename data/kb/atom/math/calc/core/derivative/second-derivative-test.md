# 📌 Atom Note: Second Derivative Test

## 🧩 Core Idea

The Second Derivative Test classifies a **critical point** using the concavity of the function (how the slope is changing), instead of tracking sign changes of $f'(x)$.

---

## 📐 Setup

Let $f(x)$ be twice differentiable. Suppose $c$ is a **critical point**, meaning:

- $f'(c) = 0$

We then evaluate the second derivative at $c$:

- $f''(c)$

---

## 🔍 Test Rule

### 📈 Local Minimum

If:

- $f''(c) > 0$

Then:

- The graph is **concave up** at $c$
- ⇒ local minimum at $c$

---

### 📉 Local Maximum

If:

- $f''(c) < 0$

Then:

- The graph is **concave down** at $c$
- ⇒ local maximum at $c$

---

### ⚪ Inconclusive

If:

- $f''(c) = 0$

Then:

- The test fails (no conclusion)
- Must use First Derivative Test or other methods

---

## 🧠 Intuition

The second derivative measures how the slope is changing:

- $f''(x) > 0$ → slopes are increasing → curve bends upward
- $f''(x) < 0$ → slopes are decreasing → curve bends downward

So the test asks:

> “Is the graph bending up or down at the critical point?”
