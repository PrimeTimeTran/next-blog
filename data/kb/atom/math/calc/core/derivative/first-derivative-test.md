# 📌 Atom Note: First Derivative Test

## 🧩 Core Idea

The First Derivative Test determines whether a **critical point** is a local maximum, local minimum, or neither by analyzing how the derivative changes sign around that point.

---

## 📐 Setup

Let $f(x)$ be a differentiable function. Suppose $c$ is a **critical point**, meaning:

- $f'(c) = 0$, or
- $f'(c)$ is undefined

We then examine the sign of $f'(x)$ **around $c$**.

---

## 🔍 Test Procedure

Check intervals:

- Left of $c$: $f'(x)$
- Right of $c$: $f'(x)$

---

## 📊 Conclusions

### 📈 Local Minimum

If:

- $f'(x) < 0$ before $c$
- $f'(x) > 0$ after $c$

Then:

- Function goes **down then up**
- ⇒ local minimum at $c$

---

### 📉 Local Maximum

If:

- $f'(x) > 0$ before $c$
- $f'(x) < 0$ after $c$

Then:

- Function goes **up then down**
- ⇒ local maximum at $c$

---

### ⚪ Neither

If:

- No sign change in $f'(x)$

Then:

- No local extremum at $c$

---

## 🧠 Intuition

The derivative tells you slope direction:

- Positive derivative → increasing function
- Negative derivative → decreasing function

So the test asks:

> “Does the function switch from increasing to decreasing (or vice versa) at this point?”
