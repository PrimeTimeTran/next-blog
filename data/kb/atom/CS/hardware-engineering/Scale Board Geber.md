A Scale Board Gerber refers to the Gerber files associated with a PCB (Printed Circuit Board) designed for a scale board. Gerber files are a standard format used in the electronics industry to describe the various layers of a PCB, including the copper layers, solder mask, silkscreen, and drill data.

A "scale board" could refer to a specific PCB that is part of a weighing scale or another application where precise measurements are essential. The Gerber files for such a board would include all the necessary information for manufacturing the PCB, ensuring it meets the design specifications for that particular scale board.

Here's what each Gerber file typically contains:

- **Copper Layers (Top and Bottom)**: Defines the electrical connections.
- **Solder Mask Layers**: Determines where solder can be applied.
- **Silkscreen Layers**: Used for text, labels, and component outlines.
- **Drill Files**: Specifies where holes are drilled for vias and component leads.
- **Outline Layer**: Shows the physical dimensions and shape of the board.

These files are essential for PCB manufacturers to fabricate the board accurately according to the design.
