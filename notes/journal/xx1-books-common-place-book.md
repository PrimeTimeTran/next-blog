# Commonplace Book

It’s not a diary or journal—it’s more like a **curated knowledge vault**. You store things worth remembering:

- Quotes
- Insights
- Concepts
- Observations
- Questions

### Purpose

The goal is to:

- Build a **second brain** of valuable thoughts
- Improve **understanding through reflection**
- Make ideas **reusable and connected**

### How It Works

Instead of writing everything chronologically, you:

1. **Capture** something interesting (from books, conversations, etc.)
2. **Organize** it (by topic, theme, or tags)
3. **Reflect** on it (add your own thoughts or interpretations)

### Key Difference from Notes

- Regular notes = storing information
- Commonplace book = **processing + curating wisdom**

### Typical Structure

- Headings or categories (e.g., “Habits,” “Philosophy,” “Business”)
- Entries under each with:

  - Quote or idea
  - Source
  - Your commentary

### Example Entry

- “We are what we repeatedly do.”
  — Aristotle
  → Consistency > intensity. This connects to habit formation.

### Why It’s Powerful

- Encourages **active thinking**, not passive reading
- Helps you **spot patterns across ideas**
- Turns scattered knowledge into **usable insight**

## How to Start and What to Include

**Choose a Format:** You can use a physical notebook, binder, or digital apps like Notion or Goodreads.

**Gather Content:** Write down quotes, recipes, poetry, song lyrics, witty phrases, or excerpts from books that resonate with you.

**Organize:** Create thematic sections, such as "on creativity" or "philosophy," or create an index at the back for easy retrieval.

**Review:** Periodically revisit your entries to connect ideas over time.

## Key Benefits

**Better Memory:** Writing things down acts as a, in the words of writer Ryan Holiday, memory aid for wisdom.

**Increased Creativity:** Collecting disparate ideas allows you to find connections between different subjects.

**Active Engagement:** Transforms reading into a productive activity.

**Personalization:** It is entirely customizable and doesn't need to be daily to be effective.
