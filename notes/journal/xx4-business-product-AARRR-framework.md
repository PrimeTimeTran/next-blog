# AARRR framework

The AARRR framework, or "Pirate Metrics" (Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue), is a five-stage model developed by Dave McClure to help startups and product teams track user behavior, optimize the customer journey, and drive data-driven growth. It focuses on actionable metrics—from how users find a product to how they generate revenue.

## The 5 Stages of AARRR

**• Acquisition** (Where do users come from?): The channels through which users discover your product (e.g., SEO, PPC, social media).

**• Activation** (Do users have a good first experience?): When a user has their "aha!" moment and realizes the product's value (e.g., signing up, completing onboarding).

**• Retention** (Do users come back?): The ability to keep users returning and engaged over time.

**• Referral** (Do users tell others?): The process of turning users into advocates who recommend the product to others.

**• Revenue** (Do users pay?): The monetization stage, measuring how the product makes money (e.g., subscriptions, purchases).

### Why Use AARRR?

**• Focus on Actionable Metrics:** It helps avoid "vanity metrics" by focusing on data that impacts business growth.

**• Identifies Bottlenecks:** It highlights where users are dropping off in the funnel, allowing for targeted optimizations.

**• Customer-Centric:** It organizes the journey around user behavior rather than internal company structure.

## Common Metrics for Each Stage

| Stage [1, 3, 4, 8, 11] | Key Metrics                                                                         |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Acquisition            | Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Click-Through Rate (CTR), Traffic Sources          |
| Activation             | Onboarding Completion Rate, Time to Value (TTV), User Activation Rate               |
| Retention              | Churn Rate, Daily/Monthly Active Users (DAU/MAU), Repeat Purchase Rate              |
| Referral               | Net Promoter Score (NPS), Viral Coefficient, Referral Rate                          |
| Revenue                | Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR), Average Order Value |
