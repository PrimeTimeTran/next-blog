## A Simple Weekly Practice Loop

Keep it tight and intentional:

**Day 1 — Forms**

- Rotated boxes, cylinders, intersections

**Day 2 — Real Object Study**

- Draw + break down an object into simple shapes

**Day 3 — Exploded View**

- Take that object → draw it disassembled

**Day 4 — Diagram Practice**

- Add arrows, labels, flow

**Day 5 — Invent Something**

- Sketch a simple idea (even dumb ones)
- Focus on clarity, not perfection

**Day 6 — Copy Good Diagrams**

- Look at manuals, patents, product sketches
- Recreate them

**Day 7 — Light / Rest**

## Goal Context

If your aim is to **diagram ideas clearly** (inventions, systems, circuits, product concepts), your training should be different from traditional art.

You’re essentially training to think like a mix of:

- Product designer
- Engineer
- Visual communicator

---

## The 5 Core Skills for Diagram Drawing

### 1. 3D Form & Perspective (Priority #1)

Focus on:

- Boxes in any rotation (not just aligned)
- Cylinders, pipes, joints
- Intersections (e.g., pipe going into a box)

**Why:**  
Everything mechanical = combinations of simple forms

👉 This is the foundation of drawing inventions

---

### 2. Object Breakdown (Reverse Engineering)

Instead of just drawing objects—**analyze them**.

Practice:

- Take something like a mouse, keyboard, or tool
- Break it into:
  - Primitives (boxes, cylinders)
  - Functional parts

**Goal:**  
“This isn’t complex → it’s 6 simple shapes combined”

---

### 3. Exploded Views & Diagrams

This is **critical** for your goal.

Practice:

- Objects pulled apart into layers
- Arrows showing movement or assembly
- Internal components

Think:

- IKEA instructions
- Patent drawings
- Engineering diagrams

---

### 4. Line Clarity & Visual Hierarchy

This separates messy sketches from clear diagrams.

Practice:

- Thick outer lines
- Thin internal detail lines
- Dashed lines (hidden parts)
- Clean, confident strokes (no chicken scratch)

**Goal:**  
Make someone understand your idea instantly

---

### 5. Annotation & Labels

Diagrams = communication tools.

Add:

- Arrows
- Labels
- Notes
- Numbered steps

Even simple sketches become powerful when labeled.

---

## What You Don’t Need Much (for This Goal)

You can de-prioritize:

- Hyper-realistic shading
- Advanced anatomy
- Detailed portraits

These don’t contribute much to diagram clarity.

## High-Impact Daily Exercise

Do this daily:

1. Pick a random object near you
2. Draw it in 3D (simple shapes only)
3. Draw it again as an exploded diagram
4. Add arrows + labels

**15–20 minutes/day = massive improvement over time**
