# TAM, SAM, and SOM

Metrics used to estimate market size and potential revenue.

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** The total global demand for a product.
- **SAM (Serviceable Available Market):** The segment of TAM within your geographical/technological reach.
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** The portion of SAM you can realistically capture.

---

# Key Differences and Definitions

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market):**
  The "big picture" or maximum potential demand for a product or service. It represents the total revenue opportunity if 100% market share were achieved.

- **SAM (Serviceable Available Market):**
  The portion of TAM that fits your current business model, geography, and product capabilities. It represents the segment you can realistically target.

- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):**
  The subset of SAM that you can realistically capture in the short term, taking into account competition, resources, and capacity. This is the most practical number for setting sales targets and informing investors of your immediate goals.

---

# Comparison Table

| Metric | Definition                    | Focus             | Question Answered                     |
| ------ | ----------------------------- | ----------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| TAM    | Total Addressable Market      | Total Opportunity | How big is the universe of potential? |
| SAM    | Serviceable Available Market  | Targetable Market | Which part of the market can I reach? |
| SOM    | Serviceable Obtainable Market | Captured Market   | What can I realistically win now?     |

---

# Why They Matter

1. **Investor Interest**
   Investors use these metrics to evaluate the viability, scalability, and growth potential of a startup.

2. **Strategic Planning**
   Helps in setting realistic sales goals, defining marketing strategies, and allocating resources effectively.

3. **Market Validation**
   Identifies if the market is large enough to warrant investment (large TAM) while ensuring the business has a realistic approach to capturing it (achievable SOM).

---

# How to Calculate (Example)

- **TAM** = Total number of potential customers worldwide × Annual contract value
- **SAM** = TAM × Percentage of market you can serve (due to language, geography, etc.)
- **SOM** = SAM × Percentage of market share you can realistically capture

---

# Example

If a company makes software for coffee shops:

- **TAM:** All coffee shops worldwide
- **SAM:** Coffee shops in North America (where they operate)
- **SOM:** The 2% of North American coffee shops they can realistically onboard this year based on their sales team size
