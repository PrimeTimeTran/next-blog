## The Four Agreements

Don Miguel Ruiz outlines a code of conduct based on ancient Toltec wisdom designed to eliminate self-limiting beliefs and achieve personal freedom, happiness, and love. The four core principles are: 1) Be impeccable with your word, 2) Don't take anything personally, 3) Don't make assumptions, and 4) Always do your best. [1, 2, 3, 4]

**• 1. Be Impeccable with Your Word**: Speak with integrity, using your words to build up rather than tear down yourself or others. Words hold power to create reality and should be used for truth and love.
**• 2. Don't Take Anything Personally:** Other people's actions and words are a reflection of their own reality, not yours. Ignoring this, and taking things personally, makes you a victim, leading to needless suffering and defensiveness.
**• 3. Don't Make Assumptions:** Find the courage to ask questions to avoid misunderstandings, sadness, and drama. Assuming that others think, feel, and act the way you do leads to false perceptions.
**• 4. Always Do Your Best:** Your "best" changes constantly depending on your health, mood, and circumstances. Simply doing your best in any given moment avoids self-judgment, regret, and guilt.

These agreements are meant to break old, fear-based, and limiting societal agreements to foster a personal "dream of heaven".

• Application: The book is frequently used for personal development, addiction recovery, and improving relationships by encouraging self-awareness and better communication.
• Key Takeaway: The book argues that you can transform your life by replacing old, limiting beliefs with these new, empowering ones.
