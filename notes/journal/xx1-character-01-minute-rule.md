The **2-Minute Rule** from James Clear is about making habits easy to start.

### Core Idea

When starting a new habit, **scale it down to something that takes 2 minutes or less**.

### Why It Works

- Reduces resistance and procrastination
- Builds consistency first, intensity later
- Helps you focus on **showing up**, not performing perfectly

### How It’s Applied

Instead of:

- “Read every night” → **Read 1 page**
- “Work out daily” → **Put on workout clothes**
- “Study for an hour” → **Open your notes**

### Key Principle

You’re not trying to complete the habit—you’re **mastering the habit of starting**.

### Deeper Insight

Small actions act as **gateway habits**. Once you begin, you often keep going—but even if you don’t, you’ve reinforced the identity of someone who shows up.

### One-Line Summary

Make habits so easy you can’t say no.
