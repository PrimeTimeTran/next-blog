# SQ4R

An active reading and studying technique designed to improve comprehension and retention of text-based material through six steps: Survey, Question, Read, Reflect, Recite, and Review. It helps transform passive reading into an engaging process, enhancing long-term memory by guiding learners to organize information, relate it to existing knowledge, and review it systematically. [1, 2, 3, 4]

## Components:

• Survey (S): Scan the text quickly before reading in detail. Look at headings, subheadings, graphs, and the summary to get an overview of the content.
• Question (Q): Turn headings and subheadings into questions to create a purpose for reading. Ask "Who," "What," "Why," or "How".
• Read (R): Actively read the text section by section to find answers to the questions formulated in the previous step.
• Reflect/Relate (R): Pause to think about the material and relate it to what you already know, or connect it to real-world examples to deepen understanding.
• Recite/Record (R): Answer the questions aloud or write down key points in your own words without looking at the text to solidify memory.
• Review (R): Go over your notes, questions, and answers to ensure you understand and can recall the material.

## Benefits and Usage

• Improves Comprehension: Ideal for difficult textbooks or dense academic material.
• Increases Retention: Helps move information into long-term memory through repetition and active engagement.
• Active Learning: Encourages critical thinking rather than just highlighting text.

SQ4R is commonly recommended for effective study habits, allowing students to maximize the return on their reading time.
