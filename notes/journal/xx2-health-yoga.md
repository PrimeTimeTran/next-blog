# Yoga Classes

Yoga classes vary from physically intense (Vinyasa, Power) to meditative (Yin, Restorative), focusing on either alignment (Iyengar) or breath-connected movement (Hatha, Flow). Popular styles include beginner-friendly Hatha, dynamic Vinyasa, hot yoga (Bikram), and restorative, slow-paced practices suitable for all levels, each offering unique physical and mental benefits. [1, 2, 3, 4]  
Popular Types of Yoga Classes

## Classes

**• Hatha:** A gentler, traditional introduction to yoga focusing on holding basic postures to build strength and balance.
**• Ashtanga:** An athletic, structured series of poses performed in a specific, rigorous order.
**• Iyengar:** Emphasizes precision and alignment, often using props like blocks, straps, and blankets, making it excellent for injury recovery.
**• Yin Yoga:** Slow and passive; poses are held for 5 minutes or more, targeting deep connective tissues.
**• Restorative Yoga:** Deeply relaxing, using props to fully support the body to de-stress the nervous system.
**• Vinyasa/Flow:** Fast-paced and dynamic, linking breath to movement for a cardiovascular, athletic workout.
**• Power Yoga:** A fitness-based approach derived from Ashtanga, focusing on strength, sweat, and cardio.
**• Hot Yoga/Bikram:** Practiced in high temperatures ($85-100^\circ$F) to increase flexibility and aid in detoxifying the body.

How to Choose

• For Beginners: Hatha, Ashtanga, Gentle Flow, Iyengar.
• For Fitness & Sweat: Vinyasa, Power, Hot Yoga.
• For Relaxation & Flexibility: Yin, Restorative.
