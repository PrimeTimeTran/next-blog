# The C's of Marketing

The 4 C's of Marketing—Consumer Wants/Needs, Cost, Convenience, and Communication—shift focus from product-centricity (4 P's) to a customer-centric approach. This model aims to improve customer satisfaction and relationship building by analyzing what the customer wants, the total cost of ownership, ease of purchase, and active dialogue.

## The 4 C's of Marketing (Lauterborn's Framework)

This framework serves as a customer-focused alternative to the traditional 4 P's (Product, Price, Place, Promotion).

1. **Consumer Wants and Needs (replaces Product)**: Instead of focusing on producing a product, the focus is on creating a solution that meets a specific consumer need.

2. **Cost to Satisfy (replaces Price)**: Rather than just the price, this considers the total cost of buying the product, including time, effort, and opportunity costs.

3. **Convenience to Buy (replaces Place)**: Focuses on making it as easy as possible for customers to find and buy the product, often including online and in-person channels.

4. **Communication (replaces Promotion)**: Rather than one-way advertising, this emphasizes two-way conversation, engagement, and building relationships with customers.

## Alternative Marketing "C" Frameworks

• **The 3 C's of Strategic Marketing (Ohmae)**: Focuses on the "strategic triangle": _Customer_, _Company_, and _Competition_.

• **The 5 C's Analysis**: Includes _Company_, _Customers_, _Competitors_, _Collaborators_, and _Climate_ (contextual factors) to conduct a thorough situation analysis.

• **The 7 C's of Marketing**: Focuses on _Customer_, _Content_, _Context_, _Community_, _Convenience_, _Consistency_, and _Conversion_.

## 4 C's vs. 4 P's

• **4 P's (Product/Seller-Focused):** _Product_, _Price_, _Place_, _Promotion_.

• **4 C's (Customer-Focused):** _Consumer_, _Cost_, _Convenience_, _Communication_.

Using the 4 C's ensures that marketing efforts are tailored to the audience's needs and behaviors rather than just selling an existing product.
