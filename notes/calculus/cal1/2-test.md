## Calculus I — Test 2: Limits & Continuity (20 practice questions)

1. Evaluate: \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to2} \frac{x^{2}-4}{x-2}\).

2. Use limit laws to compute: \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to1} (3x^{2}-2x+5)\).

3. Find the one-sided limits: \(\displaystyle\lim*{x\to0^{-}} \frac{|x|}{x}\) and \(\displaystyle\lim*{x\to0^{+}} \frac{|x|}{x}\).

4. Determine whether \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to\infty} \frac{5x^{2}-x}{2x^{2}+3}\) exists; if so, find it.

5. Evaluate (limit at infinity): \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to\infty} \frac{3x-7}{\sqrt{x^{2}+1}}\).

6. Find the vertical asymptotes (values of \(x\) where the limit is infinite) of \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{(x-1)(x+2)}\) by computing appropriate one-sided limits.

7. Determine \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to3} \frac{x-3}{(x-3)^{2}}\) and classify it as finite or infinite (and its sign from each side).

8. For the piecewise function
   \[ f(x)=\begin{cases} \dfrac{x^{2}-4}{x-2}, & x\neq 2,\\[4pt]
   10, & x=2,\end{cases} \]
   compute \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to2} f(x)\) and decide whether \(f\) is continuous at \(x=2\).

9. Using the Squeeze Theorem, evaluate \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to0} x^{2}\sin\frac{1}{x}\).

10. Compute the special trig limits (show results):
    \[ \displaystyle\lim*{x\to0} \frac{\sin x}{x} \quad\text{and}\quad \displaystyle\lim*{x\to0} \frac{1-\cos x}{x}. \]

11. Evaluate: \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to0} \frac{\tan x}{x}\).

12. Determine the following one-sided limits and conclude whether the two-sided limit exists:
    \(\displaystyle\lim*{x\to0^{-}} \frac{1}{x}\) and \(\displaystyle\lim*{x\to0^{+}} \frac{1}{x}\).

13. Evaluate: \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to\infty} \left(1+\frac{2}{x}\right)^{x}\) (state whether it converges and if so to what value).

14. Let \(f(x)=\dfrac{2x^{2}-8}{x-2}\). Compute \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to2} f(x)\) (use algebraic simplification / limit laws).

15. Determine all points of continuity for \(g(x)=\begin{cases} x^{2}, & x\le 1,\\[4pt] \dfrac{1}{x-1}, & x>1.\end{cases}\)

16. Provide the formal epsilon–delta statement and then (briefly) find a delta for the following limit:
    \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to 3} (2x+1)=7\). That is, given any \(\varepsilon>0\), find a \(\delta>0\) so that \(|(2x+1)-7|<\varepsilon\) whenever \(0<|x-3|<\delta\).

17. Compute the limit using substitution or algebra: \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to0} \frac{\sqrt{1+2x}-1}{x}\).

18. For \(h(x)=\dfrac{x^{3}-8}{x-2}\), compute \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to2} h(x)\) and interpret the result (removable discontinuity vs vertical asymptote).

19. Use limits to decide whether \(\displaystyle\lim\_{x\to0} \frac{\sin(3x)}{x} = 3\) is true; show the steps you would use (hint: substitution or known trig limit).

20. Explain briefly why if \(\lim*{x\to a} f(x)=L\) and \(\lim*{x\to a} g(x)=M\) then \(\lim\_{x\to a} (f(x)+g(x))=L+M\) (state which limit law justifies this).
